Victoria Skin Donation Project

Victoria Skin Donation by RBM

Out of all organs' donation, the idea of donating one's skin sounds the most cringeworthy to people. It is critical to inform and educate people that skin can also be donated and break taboos that surround this conversation. Skin donation is a hugely ignored initiative in India. There are 70 lakhs burn injuries in India annually, out of which 7 lakhs require hospital admission and 1.4 lakh are fatal. Of the 1.4 lakh deaths, 90,000 of those affected are women. Burn survivors require special care and treatment including isolation, and counselling to overcome the trauma. It takes years to overcome the physical, mental and emotional trauma and a painful rehabilitation process to even remotely start living a near normal life.
In a country where only 0.34 persons per million donate organs leading to illegal organ trade, organ donation should not be an example of unethical commerce; we must restore its value.
Rotary Bangalore Midtown has been working very closely with the National Burns Centre and Victo-ria Hospital based in Bangalore, for the past couple ofyears towards this cause. They work extensive-ly in sensitizing the public about the ordeals and challenges that burn survivors face. Victoria hospi-tal has one of the very few skin banks in India and has extremely skilled surgeons who work with burn survivors. To this effect, RBM organised a walkathon on June 9, 2019 in Bengaluru.

The objective of the walkathon was twofold:

  • To create awareness about burn survivors and start conversations around them.
  • To get as many skin donation pledges as possible
